Excessive Crime Prevention Patrols: Tailoring and Surveillance of Specific Individuals

Crime prevention patrols (commonly known as ‘Safety and Security Patrols’, conducted by private organisations and others) deployed nationwide are, contrary to the positive portrayal in news reports, engaging in malicious acts that violate the law, such as tailing and surveilling specific individuals, and involve numerous serious issues, including violations of the Constitution.

The ‘code of conduct’ upon which the ‘Safety and Security Patrols’ are based is commonly known as the Public Safety Ordinance. According to experts, this is a national policy whose enactment has been particularly promoted since the establishment of the Public Safety Bureau within the National Police Agency following the 1994 amendment to the Police Act.

In fact, according to records, efforts to enact these ordinances initially focused on the municipal level, followed by the prefectural level. Starting with Osaka Prefecture in 2002, they were enacted in rapid succession in Hiroshima, Shizuoka and Ibaraki, with Nara being the last in 2008. As of March 2009, 47 out of 47 prefectures have enacted such ordinances.
